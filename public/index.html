How it works

Track lifts. Hit goals.

PlateStack uses your real gym numbers to calculate your one-rep max, build a personalized AI training plan, and then automatically adjust the weights week by week based on how your workouts are actually going.

The basics, in order

1
Enter your stats and current liftsAge, bodyweight, training days per week, and what you can currently lift. We use these to estimate your 1RM (one-rep max — the most you could lift for a single rep).
2
Set your goalStrength, size (hypertrophy), endurance, athletic performance, or something specific — tell us exactly what you're chasing.
3
Get your AI-generated planA multi-week program built around your exact numbers, goal, schedule, equipment, and any injuries you list.
4
Log your workouts as you goOne tap per set. Enter actual weight/reps if different from prescribed, or just hit "Log as prescribed" and you're done.
5
The plan adapts automaticallyWeights go up when you're crushing it, stay the same when you're holding steady, and back off when you're struggling. You don't have to do the math.

What "adaptive" actually means

Most training apps give you a fixed plan. PlateStack watches what you actually lifted and adjusts the next sets and next week's weights based on it:

  • Within a workout: if one set felt way too easy or way too hard, remaining sets of that same exercise adjust immediately.
  • Week to week: when you finish a week, hit "Generate Next Week" and weights are reassessed for every exercise based on how that whole week went — bumped up, held, or deloaded.

What's RPE? (totally optional)

RPE = Rate of Perceived Exertion, a 1–10 scale for how hard a set felt:

  • RPE 6 — could have done ~4 more reps easily
  • RPE 8 — hard but had ~2 reps left in the tank
  • RPE 10 — absolute max, could not have done another rep

You can log a set without it — the plan still adapts, just based on reps alone. If you do log RPE, adjustments get smarter (big easy bumps when you've got gas left, earlier deloads when you're grinding).

Terms & privacy

Terms of Service & Privacy Policy

By using PlateStack you agree to these terms. Last updated April 2026.

1. Not medical advice

PlateStack provides general fitness information only — it is not medical advice, physical therapy, or a substitute for a qualified coach. The AI-generated training plans and automatic weight adaptations are informational suggestions based on the data you provide. Consult a qualified professional before starting any new program, especially if you have injuries, medical conditions, or are pregnant. You train at your own risk.

2. Accuracy of estimates and adaptations

1RM calculations use a weighted average of Brzycki, Lander, Epley, and Mayhew formulas. The plan adaptation engine adjusts prescribed weights based on your logged performance and self-reported effort (RPE). These are estimates — never attempt a true 1RM without a spotter, and use your own judgment to ignore or modify any prescribed weight that feels unsafe.

3. Your responsibility

You are solely responsible for how you use this information. PlateStack and its operators are not liable for any injury, damage, or loss resulting from following generated advice or adapted training prescriptions.

4. What data we collect

If you create an account: your email address, password (stored as a salted PBKDF2 hash — we never see your actual password), and any lifting data you enter (exercise names, weights, reps, RPE, notes, training plans, plan-tracking sessions). If you use the site without an account: generated plans are stored in your browser only. We also log anonymized daily visit counts using a hashed IP address that cannot be reversed to identify you.

5. How we use your data

Your lifting data is used to generate training plans, calculate estimated 1RMs, and adapt future plan prescriptions. We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with third parties for marketing. We use Anthropic's API to generate plans — when you request a plan, relevant workout context is sent to Anthropic to produce the response. Review Anthropic's privacy policy for details. Our hosting is provided by Railway.

6. Your rights (GDPR / CCPA)

You have the right to access, export, and delete your data at any time. Account holders can download a full JSON export of everything we store about them, or permanently delete their account and all associated data, from the profile menu. Deletion is immediate and irreversible. We retain anonymized aggregate usage statistics (daily visit counts) indefinitely; these cannot be linked back to you.

7. Data retention

Account data is retained for as long as your account exists. If you delete your account, all identifiable data is permanently removed within 24 hours. Session tokens older than 30 days are automatically pruned. We have no offline backups that persist deleted data.

8. Acceptable use

Do not abuse or spam the service, attempt to circumvent rate limits, or use the service to generate content unrelated to strength training. We reserve the right to block access for misuse.

9. Liability

Total liability is capped at what you have paid (zero during free beta). No warranty of fitness for a particular purpose is given or implied.

10. Contact

For data requests or questions, contact us through the email link in the footer.
